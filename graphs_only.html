Fig A: Strap Force vs Board Angle
m=100 kg, M=500 kg, p=0.75, φ=10° · dashed line = current θ=50°

Flatter board = more force on the straps. Your wall is at 50°. Blue = static, red = worst dynamic move (3× body weight), orange dashed = 3× safety factor.

Fig B: Strap Force vs Climber Position
θ=50° · position as % of board length from pivot

Higher up the board = more leverage = more force on the straps. The line doesn't start at zero because the 500 kg board is pulling on the straps all by itself, even with nobody climbing.

Fig C: Impact Force vs Stopping Distance
m=100 kg, h=0.5 m · stiffer straps = much higher forces

If someone grabs the board mid-fall, the force depends on how much the system gives before they stop. Less stretch (left side) = massive forces. More stretch (right side) = lower forces. The green dashed line is the 8 kN anchor rating. Anything above it means the anchors could fail.

Same scenarios, divided by 3 for each bolt. Red dashed = 8 kN anchor rating. Blue dashed = 3× safety factor. Above the red line = trouble.
